## Executive Summary

This essay compiles the **independent specialist evidence** that directly contradicts the psychiatric/functional framing applied to John Thomson's symptoms. Each piece of evidence comes from NHS clinicians with **no stake in any dispute** — they simply documented what they found.

The cumulative weight of this evidence establishes that:
- The patient's symptoms have **organic (physical) causes**
- The psychiatric diagnoses were **inappropriate**
- The "functional" framing has been **repeatedly refuted**

## The Central Vindication: Somatoform Removed

### Dr Gorrie's Action (July 2025)

On 22 July 2025, Dr Alexander Gorrie reviewed John Thomson's medical records and **marked the Somatoform Pain Disorder diagnosis as INACTIVE**.

His note:
> *"there were clear causes for his pain."*

#### Why This Is Significant

GPs typically do not remove psychiatric diagnoses without formal psychiatric reassessment. That Dr Gorrie removed it based solely on records review indicates:

- The error was **self-evident**
- The documented pathology made the diagnosis **untenable**
- A responsible GP **could not leave it in place**

This is internal NHS vindication — not an opinion purchased for litigation.

### Pain Clinic Refutation (2023)

The Chelsea & Westminster Pain Clinic documented:

> *"GP prev told somatic, pain clinic at C&W; felt not somatic."*

And specifically:
> *"Somatoform... NOT their opinion"*

The specialists managing the patient's pain explicitly rejected the psychiatric framing imposed by the GP.

## Imaging Vindication: Thoracic Outlet Syndrome

### Six Years of Documented Pathology

Thoracic Outlet Syndrome (TOS) has been documented on imaging across a **six-year timeline**:

| Date | Study | Finding |
|------|-------|---------|
| June 2019 | Duplex Ultrasound | "Marked haemodynamic changes... positive result" |
| January 2024 | MR Thoracic Inlet | "Compression of subclavian artery and vein" |
| April 2024 | CT Aorta | "TOS cannot be excluded" |
| June 2025 | Doppler | "Subclavian veins compressed with no flow" |
| July 2024 | MRA | "Confirms compression and focal stenosis" |

### The 2019 Study

The first positive imaging occurred in **June 2019** — before any psychiatric framing existed:

> *"Complete cessation of flow within the distal subclavian artery during Adson's and EAST manoeuvres... a positive result."*

This objective finding predates the entire narrative.

### The 2025 Doppler

Six years later, the same pathology persists:

> *"At 90° abduction, subclavian veins compressed with no flow"*

This demonstrates continuous organic pathology over the entire period during which the patient was being told his symptoms were psychological.

### Vascular Surgeon Confirmation (July 2024)

Mr Aaron Goh, Vascular Surgeon at Royal Free Hospital, formally listed:

> *"Thoracic outlet syndrome (neurogenic), subclavian steal syndrome, vertebral artery compression"*

A consultant vascular surgeon has documented multiple **organic diagnoses** for the symptoms that GPs attributed to somatoform disorder.

## Neurological Vindication

- The patient requires ongoing **antiepileptic treatment**
- **NEAD (Non-Epileptic Attack Disorder)** has been ruled out

Any suggestion that the seizures were "functional" or psychological is refuted by formal neurological diagnosis.
