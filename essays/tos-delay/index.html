Thoracic Outlet Syndrome: The Ongoing Delay

Six Years of Documented Vascular Pathology

Date: 2026-01-18
Status: CURRENT
Source: FOUNDATIONAL_ESSAY_TOS_DELAY_2026-01-18.docx

Executive Summary

Documents the 6+ year timeline of Thoracic Outlet Syndrome from first positive Doppler in June 2019 through subsequent confirmatory imaging in 2024 and 2025. Despite objective evidence of vascular compression, the condition has not received definitive surgical intervention. Patient symptom severity documented: 'would rather his arm be amputated.'

Key Arguments

1

First positive Doppler June 2019 - 'marked haemodynamic changes'

2

2024 MR confirms dynamic subclavian compression

3

2025 Doppler: bilateral subclavian veins 'compressed with no flow'

4

Radiologist: 'TOS cannot be excluded' with static imaging

5

MRA confirms compression and focal stenosis

6

Specialist documents extreme suffering - patient prefers amputation

7

6+ years of documented pathology without definitive intervention

Supporting Evidence

F032 VINDICATION

2019 Duplex Ultrasound Shows TOS-Positive Result

Bilateral upper-limb arterial duplex study recorded complete cessation of flow within the distal subclavian artery during Adson's and EAST manoeuvres bilaterally. Conclusion: 'Marked haemodynamic changes… a positive result.'
F033 VINDICATION

2024 UCLH MR Reports Dynamic Subclavian Compression

UCLH MR thoracic inlet/outlet report describes compression of left subclavian artery and vein with arms abducted, including apparent focal stenosis.
F036 VINDICATION

US Doppler June 2025 Shows Dynamic Bilateral Subclavian Occlusion

Royal Free ultrasound Doppler documents dynamic vascular compression. Both arms at 90° abduction: subclavian veins 'compressed with no flow'. Right arm in Surrender position: both artery and vein 'compressed with no flow detected'.
F037 VINDICATION

CT Aorta April 2024: TOS Not Excluded

UCLH CT Aorta performed with arms down shows no stenosis in that position but explicitly states 'A thoracic outlet syndrome affecting left subclavian artery cannot be excluded.'
F357 VINDICATION

MRA Confirms Subclavian Compression and Stenosis

MRA imaging confirms 'compression of the subclavian vein on the left as well as a focal stenosis of the left subclavian artery' in the subclavicular space.
