The 10-Month Diagnostic Delay

How T5-T8 Vertebral Fractures Went Undiagnosed Despite Red Flag Presentation

Documents the 10-month delay in diagnosing T5-T8 vertebral fractures from the December 2021 seizure until MRI confirmation in October 2022. Initial A&E assessment dismissed the injury as 'soft tissue' despite NICE NG59 red flag presentation. The delay was compounded by imaging the wrong spinal region (lumbar instead of thoracic).

Key Arguments

  • NICE NG59 red flags present but not actioned
  • Initial A&E dismissed injury as 'soft tissue'
  • Wrong spinal region imaged (lumbar MRI when thoracic was symptomatic)

Medication Causation: Pregabalin Withdrawal and Seizure

How Abrupt Pregabalin Reduction Led to the Index Seizure Event

Documents the causation chain linking abrupt pregabalin reduction (600mg to 100mg) in October 2021 to the seizure event on 23 December 2021. BNF guidance mandates gradual withdrawal over minimum 1 week. Dr Simeoni documented: 'When had a seizure pregabalin had been stopped abruptly.' The patient now has a formal epilepsy diagnosis.

Key Arguments

  • BNF mandates gradual pregabalin withdrawal - not followed
  • Reduction from 600mg to 100mg in 2-3 days
  • Independent specialist documented seizure occurred when pregabalin stopped abruptly
  • +3 more arguments

Somatoform Contamination: The Covert Diagnosis

How a Psychiatric Label Was Applied Without Assessment, Knowledge, or Basis
