Medication Causation: Pregabalin Withdrawal and Seizure

How Abrupt Pregabalin Reduction Led to the Index Seizure Event

Date: 2026-01-18
Status: CURRENT
Source: FOUNDATIONAL_ESSAY_MEDICATION_CAUSATION_2026-01-18.docx

Executive Summary

Documents the causation chain linking abrupt pregabalin reduction (600mg to 100mg) in October 2021 to the seizure event on 23 December 2021. BNF guidance mandates gradual withdrawal over minimum 1 week. Dr Simeoni documented: 'When had a seizure pregabalin had been stopped abruptly.' The patient now has a formal epilepsy diagnosis.

Key Arguments

1

BNF mandates gradual pregabalin withdrawal - not followed

2

Reduction from 600mg to 100mg in 2-3 days

3

Independent specialist documented seizure occurred when pregabalin stopped abruptly

4

Sleep deprivation (14+ months) lowered seizure threshold

5

Patient later diagnosed with epilepsy (not dissociative/functional seizures)

6

Pattern repeated: GP stopped OxyContin unsafely despite Pain Clinic warning of seizure risk

Supporting Evidence

F042 BREACH

Pregabalin Reduction Contrary to BNF

Pregabalin reduced from 600mg to 100mg daily between 18-20 October 2021 without proper tapering protocol. BNF mandates gradual withdrawal. Occurred ~10 weeks before December 2021 seizure.
F050 CAUSATION

Sleep Deprivation → Seizure → Fracture Chain

Causation chain: Undertreated back pain → Inability to lie flat → 14+ months sleep deprivation → Lowered seizure threshold → Tonic-clonic seizure 23 Dec 2021 → T5-T8 vertebral fractures.
F324 CAUSATION

Pregabalin Cessation → Seizure Causation Documented

Dr Simeoni documented: 'When had a seizure pregabalin had been stopped abruptly.' Direct documentation of causal link.
F334 MEDICATION

GP Stopped OxyContin Unsafely - Seizure Risk

Pain Clinic explicitly documented: 'abruptly stopping oxycontin is unsafe as the patient is at risk of seizures and is at high risk of Withdrawal.'
F354 VINDICATION

Formal Epilepsy Diagnosis

Dr Karunaratne formally diagnosed: 'Epilepsy with generalised seizures.' Not functional/dissociative seizures.
