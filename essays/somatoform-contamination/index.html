How a Psychiatric Label Applied Without Assessment Corrupted the Care Pathway

THOMSON v NHS PROVIDERS

Compiled: 17 January 2026 (CORRECTED 18 January 2026)

Document Status: FOUNDATIONAL EVIDENTIAL COMPILATION

SUBJECT TO LEGAL PROFESSIONAL PRIVILEGE

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document establishes how a Somatoform Pain Disorder diagnosis was applied to John Thomson's medical records on 5 January 2023 without psychiatric assessment, during an unrelated telephone consultation about opioid medication, using the wrong ICD-10 diagnostic code, without the patient's knowledge or consent. The diagnosis subsequently contaminated the care pathway, causing other clinicians to dismiss objective physical findings as psychological in origin.

The diagnosis was ultimately removed on 22 July 2025 by Dr Alexander Gorrie, who noted "there were clear causes for his pain." The removal was performed without formal psychiatric reassessment because the error was self-evident. Multiple independent specialists had already refuted the psychiatric characterisation.

THE CORE PROBLEM

Diagnosis Applied: Somatoform Pain Disorder (5 January 2023)

Applied By: Dr Bulale Ali (GP)

Context: Telephone consultation about opioid medication switching

Psychiatric Assessment: NONE conducted

ICD-10 Code Used: E278 (endocrine disorder) - INCORRECT

Correct Code: F45.4 (Persistent somatoform pain disorder)

Patient Informed: NO - discovered years later

Diagnosis Removed: 22 July 2025 by Dr Gorrie

PART ONE: THE SOMATOFORM APPLICATION

1.1 The 5 January 2023 Telephone Consultation

On 5 January 2023, John Thomson had a telephone consultation with Dr Bulale Ali at his GP practice. The documented purpose of this consultation was to discuss switching between opioid medications. During this call, without any psychiatric assessment or discussion with the patient, Dr Bulale added a Somatoform Pain Disorder diagnosis to the patient's medical record (Entry 2023-011).

Critical Timeline Context

The somatoform diagnosis was applied AFTER objective evidence of physical pathology had already been established:

SOMATOFORM DIAGNOSIS APPLIED - 3 months AFTER fractures confirmed

Breach Assessment: The application of a psychiatric diagnosis for pain AFTER objective organic pathology (vertebral fractures) had been confirmed on imaging represents a fundamental breach of clinical standards. Somatoform pain disorder is a diagnosis of exclusion; it cannot properly be applied when known physical causes for pain exist.

1.2 The Wrong Diagnostic Code

The somatoform diagnosis was coded as E278 in the GP record. E278 is the ICD-10 code for "Other specified disorders of adrenal gland" - an endocrine disorder completely unrelated to psychiatric conditions. The correct code for Persistent Somatoform Pain Disorder is F45.4.

Finding Reference: F041 - The use of the wrong ICD-10 code suggests either: (a) unfamiliarity with the diagnosis being applied, or (b) inappropriate origin of the diagnostic entry. Either explanation undermines the clinical validity of the diagnosis.

PART TWO: THE CONTAMINATION EFFECT

2.1 How Psychiatric Labels Propagate

Once a psychiatric diagnosis enters a patient's medical record, it creates a contamination effect. Subsequent clinicians, reading referral letters and historical summaries, are primed to interpret physical symptoms through a psychological lens. This is particularly problematic when the original diagnosis was applied without proper assessment.

The Pre-Existing Contamination: September 2020

The 5 January 2023 somatoform diagnosis built upon earlier psychiatric framing. On 28 September 2020, Dr Amit Gupta submitted a referral to adult secondary mental health services that included the following language:

The referral describes symptoms "hard to explain," references the patient's brother's suicide, and explicitly links family health beliefs "and in this instance somatisation."

— MH1 Referral Form, Dr Amit Gupta, 28 September 2020 (F002)

Finding Reference: F002 - This document establishes that psychiatric framing existed in GP-originated records over TWO YEARS before the formal somatoform diagnosis was applied, and over ONE YEAR before the seizure and fractures occurred. The pattern of psychological attribution was established before any of the key injury events.

A parallel labelling issue occurred with Functional Neurological Disorder (FND). The timeline evidence shows FND assessment was initially rejected, the label propagated through records, and FND tremor assessment was eventually conducted by Dr Coebergh and Dr Charlesworth (though disputed). Unlike FND tremor (assessed but disputed), Somatoform was NEVER assessed. See FND_SOMATOFORM_CORRECTION_DOCUMENT_2026-01-18.docx for full analysis.

2.3 The POTS False Positive

The pattern of applying labels without proper investigation extended to other conditions. The patient had been "labelled as POTS" (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome), but when cardiologist Dr Singhal actually reviewed the evidence:

"You were previously labelled as POTS, but the test did not show this."

— Dr Singhal, Cardiologist, 10 August 2022 (F319)

Finding Reference: F319 - This demonstrates the pattern: labels applied without proper testing, later refuted when actual investigations are performed. The POTS label, like the somatoform and FND labels, contributed to the overall picture of a patient with "functional" or "psychological" conditions - a picture that was false.

PART THREE: THE WEAPONISATION OF FAMILY TRAGEDY

3.1 The Context: Dorian Thomson's Death

John Thomson's older brother, Dorian Thomson, died by suicide in 2011 following years of undertreated chronic pain from Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome. Dorian's GP had written that "the problems were all in his mind." This family tragedy - a death caused in part by psychiatric dismissal of physical symptoms - was subsequently weaponised to justify psychiatric dismissal of John's physical symptoms.

The Tragic Irony

The healthcare providers used Dorian's death to create a narrative that John was "following the same path" - presenting psychological symptoms disguised as physical complaints. In reality, they were replicating the exact pattern that contributed to Dorian's death: dismissing objective physical symptoms as psychological.

3.2 Pain Referral Rejection: October 2021

On 14 October 2021, Dr Bhaskar rejected a Pain Clinic referral for John Thomson. The letter cited the mother's concern about suicide risk as a reason to DENY treatment:

"I do not think this patient would benefit from a referral to secondary care services"

"If his mother were concerned about suicide risk, then I would advise contacting the liaison psychiatric team... I think it is not advisable to review this patient in the Pain clinic"

— Dr Bhaskar, Pain Consultant, 14 October 2021 (F344)

Finding Reference: F344 - A family's legitimate concern about suicide risk (in the context of a brother who died by suicide after undertreated pain) was used as a reason to DENY pain treatment. This is clinically and ethically indefensible. The appropriate response to suicide concern is enhanced support, not care withdrawal.

3.3 The Pattern: Family History as Dismissal Tool

Multiple entries in the medical records reference the brother's suicide in contexts that suggest it is being used to frame John's symptoms as psychological:

September 2020 MH1 referral explicitly links "family health beliefs" and "somatisation" (F002)

October 2021 pain referral rejection cites mother's suicide concern (F344)

August 2023 phone call references brother's suicide in context of care decisions (F-GPT-XXX)

Finding Reference: F010 - The weaponisation of Dorian's death to justify non-treatment of John represents a fundamental breach of medical ethics. It demonstrates bad faith pattern, not clinical error. May support aggravated damages.

PART FOUR: THE VINDICATION

4.1 Dr Gorrie Removes the Somatoform Diagnosis

On 22 July 2025, Dr Alexander Gorrie marked the Somatoform Pain Disorder diagnosis as INACTIVE. His clinical note:

"There were clear causes for his pain."

— Dr Alexander Gorrie, 22 July 2025 (F030)

This statement is pivotal. It represents internal NHS vindication - a subsequent GP independently determining that the psychiatric diagnosis was inappropriate. Dr Gorrie did not require a formal psychiatric reassessment to remove the diagnosis because the error was self-evident from the documented organic pathology.

4.2 The Comprehensive Vindication Statement

Dr Karunaratne, the patient's epilepsy specialist, provided the most comprehensive vindication statement on 5 December 2023:

"Today was the first time he sounded somewhat positive in a very long time. This is largely due to the fact that medical explanations have been found for symptoms that he has been experiencing for a long time."

"Rib and thoracic fractures have been confirmed on a MRI scan."

"Following surgery at the Eastman dental unit, the eye symptoms are 99% better."

— Dr Karunaratne, Epilepsy Specialist, 5 December 2023 (F337, CL019)

Finding Reference: F337/F349 - "Medical explanations have been found" destroys the psychosomatic framing. This statement, from an NHS consultant, confirms that the symptoms attributed to somatoform disorder had organic causes all along.

CONCLUSIONS

The somatoform contamination pathway demonstrates a systematic pattern of diagnostic overshadowing:

Psychiatric labels were applied without proper psychiatric assessment;

These labels propagated through the medical record, contaminating subsequent clinical encounters;

Objective physical findings were dismissed as psychological/functional;

Family tragedy (brother's suicide) was weaponised to justify the psychiatric framing;

Multiple organic pathologies were eventually confirmed, vindicating the patient;

The somatoform diagnosis was removed by subsequent NHS clinician without requiring formal reassessment because the error was self-evident.
