The Vindication Evidence

Independent Specialist Confirmations That Refute Psychiatric Framing

Date: 2026-01-17
Status: CURRENT

Executive Summary

This essay compiles the independent specialist evidence that directly contradicts the psychiatric/functional framing applied to John Thomson’s symptoms. Each piece of evidence comes from NHS clinicians with no stake in any dispute — they simply documented what they found.

The cumulative weight of this evidence establishes that:

  • The patient’s symptoms have organic (physical) causes
  • The psychiatric diagnoses were inappropriate
  • The “functional” framing has been repeatedly refuted

The Central Vindication: Somatoform Removed

Dr Gorrie’s Action (July 2025)

On 22 July 2025, Dr Alexander Gorrie reviewed John Thomson’s medical records and marked the Somatoform Pain Disorder diagnosis as INACTIVE.

His note:

“there were clear causes for his pain.”

Why This Is Significant

GPs typically do not remove psychiatric diagnoses without formal psychiatric reassessment. That Dr Gorrie removed it based solely on records review indicates:

  • The error was self-evident
  • The documented pathology made the diagnosis untenable
  • A responsible GP could not leave it in place

This is internal NHS vindication — not an opinion purchased for litigation.

Pain Clinic Refutation (2023)

The Chelsea & Westminster Pain Clinic documented:

“GP prev told somatic, pain clinic at C&W; felt not somatic.”

And specifically:

“Somatoform… NOT their opinion”

The specialists managing the patient’s pain explicitly rejected the psychiatric framing imposed by the GP.

Imaging Vindication: Thoracic Outlet Syndrome

Six Years of Documented Pathology

Thoracic Outlet Syndrome (TOS) has been documented on imaging across a six-year timeline:

DateStudyFinding
June 2019Duplex Ultrasound”Marked haemodynamic changes… positive result”
January 2024MR Thoracic Inlet”Compression of subclavian artery and vein”
April 2024CT Aorta”TOS cannot be excluded”
June 2025Doppler”Subclavian veins compressed with no flow”
July 2024MRA”Confirms compression and focal stenosis”

The 2019 Study

The first positive imaging occurred in June 2019 — before any psychiatric framing existed:

“Complete cessation of flow within the distal subclavian artery during Adson’s and EAST manoeuvres… a positive result.”

This objective finding predates the entire narrative.

The 2025 Doppler

Six years later, the same pathology persists:

“At 90° abduction, subclavian veins compressed with no flow”

This demonstrates continuous organic pathology over the entire period during which the patient was being told his symptoms were psychological.

Vascular Surgeon Confirmation (July 2024)

Mr Aaron Goh, Vascular Surgeon at Royal Free Hospital, formally listed:

“Thoracic outlet syndrome (neurogenic), subclavian steal syndrome, vertebral artery compression”

A consultant vascular surgeon has documented multiple organic diagnoses for the symptoms that GPs attributed to somatoform disorder.

Neurological Vindication

Formal Epilepsy Diagnosis

In December 2024, Imperial College Healthcare neurology formally diagnosed epilepsy.

This is critical because:

  • The December 2021 seizure was genuine, not dissociative
  • The patient requires ongoing antiepileptic treatment
  • NEAD (Non-Epileptic Attack Disorder) has been ruled out

Any suggestion that the seizures were “functional” or psychological is refuted by formal neurological diagnosis.

Dr Simeoni’s Documentation

Dr Simeoni documented:

“When had a seizure pregabalin had been stopped abruptly”

This independent specialist recognised the causal link between medication withdrawal and seizure — supporting the organic causation narrative.

Psychiatric Vindication

Dr Gupta’s Assessment (2023)

A formal psychiatric assessment by Dr Gupta found:

NO: PTSD, depression, psychosis, personality disorder, or somatisation disorder

The patient underwent formal psychiatric evaluation and was found to have no psychiatric disorders.

This directly contradicts the GP-applied Somatoform diagnosis, which was entered without any psychiatric assessment.

Cardiac Vindication

POTS Diagnosis Refuted

At one point, the patient was labelled with POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome) — another functional diagnosis.

Cardiologist review found:

“Tilt table test did not show this”

Another “functional” label removed based on objective testing.

The Pattern of Vindication

Consistent Theme

Every independent specialist who has examined John Thomson has:

  1. Found organic pathology, or
  2. Refuted functional/psychiatric diagnoses, or
  3. Both

Not a single independent specialist has supported the psychiatric framing.

Legal Significance

What This Proves

The independent vindication evidence establishes:

  1. The symptoms were real — documented organic pathology
  2. The psychiatric diagnoses were wrong — independently removed/refuted
  3. The patient was telling the truth — his reports match objective findings
  4. The GP narrative was false — no independent support

Expert Evidence

These vindication findings are not expert opinions commissioned for litigation. They are:

  • Contemporaneous NHS clinical documentation
  • Independent specialist assessments
  • Objective imaging studies

They carry significant evidential weight because they were not created for legal purposes.

Conclusion

The vindication evidence in this case is overwhelming:

  • 22+ findings from independent specialists
  • 6 years of documented imaging pathology
  • Multiple diagnoses removed by subsequent clinicians
  • Zero independent support for the psychiatric framing

John Thomson’s symptoms were real. The organic pathology was real. The psychiatric diagnoses were inappropriate and have been systematically refuted by independent clinical evidence.

The defence cannot argue with NHS clinicians who independently documented what they found.
