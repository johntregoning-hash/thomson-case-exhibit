EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document compiles the independent specialist evidence that refutes the psychiatric characterisation applied to John Thomson by his former GP practice. These vindication statements come from NHS consultants, independent clinicians, and objective imaging - sources that rank higher in the evidence hierarchy than GP entries.

The vindication is comprehensive: the patient's current GP has removed the somatoform diagnosis, his epilepsy specialist has confirmed "medical explanations have been found," cardiologists have refuted the POTS label, neurologists have confirmed he was never assessed for FND, and objective imaging has demonstrated structural pathology including vertebral fractures and vascular compression.

PART ONE: THE GP VINDICATION

1.1 Dr Alexander Gorrie: Removal of Somatoform Diagnosis

Dr Alexander Gorrie is John Thomson's current GP. On 22 July 2025, he took the significant step of marking the Somatoform Pain Disorder diagnosis as INACTIVE in the patient's medical record.

"There were clear causes for his pain."

— Dr Alexander Gorrie, GP, 22 July 2025

Finding Reference: F030 | Evidence Status: VERIFIED

Significance of This Action

This represents internal NHS vindication with several critical characteristics:

The diagnosis was removed by an NHS GP, not an external expert commissioned for litigation

No formal psychiatric reassessment was required - the error was self-evident from the documented organic pathology

The action was taken based on clinical judgment, not patient request

It implicitly acknowledges that the original diagnosis was inappropriate

1.2 Dr Gorrie's Additional Statements

Dr Gorrie has made additional statements that support the vindication narrative:

"Sketchy Stuff" Reference (F021): Dr Gorrie verbally referenced "sketchy stuff" that practice manager Clive Polles had done which blocked a recent practice merger. This statement indicates awareness of problematic conduct at the former practice.

PART TWO: SPECIALIST REFUTATIONS

2.1 Dr Karunaratne: "Medical Explanations Have Been Found"

Dr Kushan Karunaratne is an NHS epilepsy specialist who has treated John Thomson since his seizures began. His clinic letter of 5 December 2023 (CL019) contains the most comprehensive vindication statement in the evidence base:

"Today was the first time he sounded somewhat positive in a very long time. This is largely due to the fact that medical explanations have been found for symptoms that he has been experiencing for a long time."

"Rib and thoracic fractures have been confirmed on a MRI scan."

"Following surgery at the Eastman dental unit, the eye symptoms are 99% better."

— Dr Kushan Karunaratne, Epilepsy Specialist, 5 December 2023

Finding References: F337, F349 | Evidence Status: VERIFIED

"Data Manipulation" Statement

Dr Karunaratne verbally used the phrase "data manipulation" when discussing GP care issues with the patient's parents present as witnesses.

Finding Reference: F020 - This statement from an NHS consultant specialist raises explicit concerns about the integrity of the GP records. The presence of the patient's parents as witnesses creates corroboration. Formal witness statements are required.

2.2 Dr Singhal: POTS Diagnosis Refuted

On 10 August 2022, cardiologist Dr Singhal reviewed the objective test results and refuted the POTS label that had been applied to the patient:

"You were previously labelled as POTS, but the test did not show this."

— Dr Singhal, Cardiologist, 10 August 2022 (CL074)

Finding Reference: F319 | Evidence Status: VERIFIED

This statement demonstrates the pattern: diagnostic labels applied without proper testing, later refuted when objective investigations are performed. POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome) has specific diagnostic criteria that were not met.

2.3 Dr Simeoni: FND Never Formally Assessed

On 29 August 2022, neurologist Dr Simeoni documented that despite the FND label appearing in the patient's records, formal assessment had never occurred:

"Has never been reviewed by the FND team at St George's."

— Dr Simeoni, Neurologist, 29 August 2022

Finding Reference: F323 | Evidence Status: VERIFIED

The FND referral to NHNN had been REJECTED due to staffing constraints on 16 June 2021 (F181). Yet the FND label continued to propagate through the medical records, contaminating subsequent clinical encounters. This represents a label without assessment.

2.4 Chelsea & Westminster Pain Clinic Refutation

The Chelsea & Westminster Pain Clinic explicitly disagreed with the somatoform characterisation. This is documented in the GP record entry of 3 May 2023 by Dr McGlone:

GP Record Quote: "GP prev told somatic, pain clinic at C&W; felt not somatic."

Finding Reference: F031 - An independent specialist pain clinic refuted the somatoform diagnosis. This demonstrates that the psychiatric characterisation was not accepted by specialists who actually assessed the patient's pain.

PART THREE: OBJECTIVE IMAGING VINDICATION

The strongest vindication comes from Tier 1 evidence: objective imaging that cannot be attributed to psychological factors. These findings demonstrate structural pathology that was present while the patient's symptoms were being dismissed as somatoform.

3.1 T5-T8 Vertebral Fractures (MRI October 2022)

The MRI performed on 16 October 2022 confirmed T5-T8 vertebral fractures. This is objective structural pathology that fully explains the thoracic pain that had been dismissed as functional or somatoform.

3.3 SCDS - Surgery With 99% Resolution

Superior Canal Dehiscence Syndrome (SCDS) was eventually diagnosed and surgically treated. Dr Karunaratne documents: "Following surgery at the Eastman dental unit, the eye symptoms are 99% better."

The near-complete resolution with surgical intervention proves the symptoms had an organic structural cause, not a psychological one. Functional symptoms do not resolve with structural surgery.

PART FOUR: PRE-SEIZURE VINDICATION

4.1 Sumeer Bhamm: Independent Documentation of GP Dismissal

Sumeer Bhamm, Senior MSK Physiotherapist at Charing Cross Hospital, conducted a face-to-face assessment on 4 August 2021 - four months BEFORE the seizure and fractures. His documentation is critical because it establishes the dismissive pattern predated the key injury events.

"He has had issues with the GP in the past, as they did not believe his pain and accused him for attention seeking."

— Sumeer Bhamm, Senior MSK Physiotherapist, 4 August 2021

Finding Reference: F022 | Evidence Status: VERIFIED

Significance of Pre-Seizure Documentation

This documentation is significant because:

It is contemporaneous - written at the time, not retrospectively;

It is independent - from an NHS physiotherapist, not the patient;

It predates the seizure and fractures - establishing the pattern existed before the key injury;

It involved physical examination - one of the few documented face-to-face assessments;

It documents the patient's perspective being dismissed - "attention seeking" is not a clinical diagnosis.

CONCLUSIONS

The vindication evidence compiled in this document establishes:

Internal NHS vindication: The patient's current GP independently removed the somatoform diagnosis because "there were clear causes for his pain";

Specialist vindication: Multiple NHS consultants refuted the psychiatric characterisation, including the epilepsy specialist who stated "medical explanations have been found";

Imaging vindication: Objective Tier 1 evidence confirms structural pathology including vertebral fractures and vascular compression;

Label refutation: POTS was refuted by cardiology testing, FND was never formally assessed, somatoform was rejected by the Pain Clinic;

Pre-existing pattern: Independent documentation establishes that the dismissive attitude predated the seizure and fractures.

This vindication evidence comes from sources that rank higher in the evidence hierarchy than the GP entries that applied the psychiatric labels. The vindication is not litigation-driven expert opinion, but internal NHS clinical recognition that the patient's symptoms had organic causes all along.
